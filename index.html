Frequently Asked Questions

Our event is in a public park. Do we need a permit for the dumpster?

In most cases, yes. Placing a roll off dumpster on public property like a park or street typically requires a permit from the local municipality. We can advise you on what the local requirements are, but the event organizer is usually responsible for securing the necessary permit before delivery.

Can I put heavy materials like dirt and sod in any of your dumpsters?

While you can, it's crucial to inform us at the time of booking. Heavy debris like concrete, dirt, rock, and sod can only be placed in our smaller dumpsters (typically a 20 yd roll off dumpster or smaller) and cannot be filled to the top due to strict road weight limits. Never mix heavy debris with general trash.

As a property manager, can I set up a recurring or on-call service for multiple properties?

Absolutely. We specialize in creating custom roll off dumpster packages for commercial property managers. We can establish an account for you that allows for easy, on-call ordering for any of your properties, with consolidated billing options available.

How do you protect the surface where the dumpster is placed?

While we are not liable for surface damage, we always recommend that customers place large wooden planks or plywood on the spot where the roll off dumpster will be set down. This helps distribute the weight and provides a protective barrier for your driveway, parking lot, or street.

What is the most common mistake people make when renting a dumpster for a landscaping project?

Underestimating the weight and volume of soil. Dirt is incredibly heavy and expands in volume when it's dug up. It's essential to discuss the scope of your excavation with us so we can ensure you get the right size bin and understand the weight restrictions.
